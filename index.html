Our Manifesto

We Invest in Those
Who Wire the Future

A manifesto is a promise. What follows is ours — the set of convictions that govern every investment we make, every founder we back, every dollar we deploy. These are not opinions we hold lightly. They are the reason Velocia exists.

Steel and wires built the last century. Code and AI are building the next. Tomorrow's infrastructure will be software-defined, AI-operated, and self-optimizing. The companies shipping that transformation own the next decade.
Generalist capital was a luxury of cheap money. That era is over. InfraTech demands exceptional focus — deep domain expertise is the prerequisite, not the differentiator. In a world of generalists, depth is the only edge that compounds.
We back companies you can't turn off. Software so embedded in operations that replacing it is unthinkable. Customer lock-in measured in years, not months. In a world where most moats evaporate in a downturn, mission-critical adoption is the only defensibility that lasts.
Infrastructure outlasts every application built on top of it. Cisco outlasted Pets.com. The grid outlasted GE. AWS outlasted Heroku. Bet on the layer that doesn't go out of style — the one that earns a toll on every transaction crossing it.
Every constraint in the AI stack is a category waiting to be born. Yesterday's compute ceiling built Nvidia. Tomorrow's energy, cooling, and orchestration ceilings will build its successors. We invest where the bottleneck is sharpest — because the hardest problem in a stack is also its most valuable.
The last decade of venture optimized for paper markups that rarely turned into distributions. We underwrite founders obsessed with generating cash and compounding it — because real revenue is the only signal that doesn't need a follow-on round to prove itself.
A model alone is inert. Intelligence scales only when power, silicon, networks, cooling, orchestration, and the software binding them work in concert. Break a single layer and the stack goes dark. We invest across the substrate — not the surface.
We don't fund optimism.
We fund inevitability.
